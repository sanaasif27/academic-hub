I am **Dr. Sana Asif**, an Assistant Professor of English and Digital Humanities
at Xavier University of Punjab. My work sits where literary studies meets
computation: distant reading and its critics, textual editing and markup, the
politics of the archive, and the futures of a critical, postcolonial digital
humanities.

## Research

My current research asks how the methods of the digital humanities — treating
text as data, encoding editions, mapping literary worlds — can be turned back
into genuinely *literary* questions rather than away from them. I am especially
interested in South Asian literature, the archive, and what computation does and
does not let us see.

## Teaching

I teach graduate and undergraduate courses in English literature and the Digital
Humanities, including a six-unit *Introduction to the Digital Humanities* for MA
English. I build my courses to be browser-only and installation-free so students
can do real hands-on work without a technical barrier.

## Selected interests

- Distant reading & computational literary study
- Textual editing, TEI, and the digital scholarly edition
- The archive, its politics, and minimal computing
- Critical, feminist, and postcolonial digital humanities
