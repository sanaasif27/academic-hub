This is the first post on my new site, so let me say plainly what it is for.

For a long time my writing, my course handouts, and the teaching materials I share
with colleagues lived in scattered folders, email attachments, and learning-management
systems that students lose access to the moment a term ends. I wanted one stable,
public place that I control.

So this site has three jobs:

1. **Writing** — essays and shorter notes on literature and the digital humanities.
2. **Teaching** — handouts and readings my current students can download any time, with no login.
3. **Resources** — teaching packs and templates colleagues are welcome to adapt.

## Why a "static" site

There is no database here and no software to keep patched — the pages are just
files. That makes the site fast, hard to break into, and cheap (free, in fact) to
host. When I want to publish something, I open a simple editor in my browser, write,
and click *Publish*; a minute later it is live. It feels like WordPress to write,
but there is far less that can go wrong.
